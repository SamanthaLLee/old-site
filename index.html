Hello! I am Samantha L. Lee, a sophomore at Rutgers University–New Brunswick pursuing a double major in Computer Science and Cognitive Science. I have a passion for learning and teaching alike, and you can find me at the Rutgers Learning Centers as an Honors Program Peer Tutor or at the InfoSeeking Lab, where I develop data collection software that helps researchers conduct information retrieval studies.

As a Douglass woman, I am a huge proponent for social justice and representation. I am the graphics chair of Women in Computer Science and the Public Relations Officer of Douglass-DIMACS Computing Corps, a club affiliated with Girls Who Code College Loops. Moreover, I am a peer mentor in the all-women Computer Science Living-Learning Community.


I am actively seeking Summer 2020 internships in technology and new research opportunities.

In my spare time, I like completing DIY projects and various crafts. Sewing, metalworking, 3D printing, folding origami — anything goes. The Makerspace has become one of my favorite places on campus.

I very much enjoy active recreation. In the past, I used to run cross-country and also used to ice skate. As of late, I have taken to badminton and penny boarding.

I owe my passion for CS to the hit comedy-drama of the 2010s, Glee. At 11 years old, I started a Tumblr blog about the show. Dissatisfied with the default theme, I learned CSS and HTML to create my own. One thing led to another, and here I am today.
